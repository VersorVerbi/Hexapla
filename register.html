We don't have password requirements, but we do have recommendations.
  1. Use a password manager--KeePass, LastPass, 1Password, Dashlane, Bitwarden, something.
  2. Failing that, more characters is always better and more types of characters is always better.
    Here's some quick math: on average, a dedicated hacker using a moderately-sized botnet can test one password in ~1.7 × 10-12 seconds, that is, 1.7 picoseconds (it takes 1000 picoseconds to get to a nanosecond, and a million nanoseconds to get to a millisecond). Discarding some other factors (like how common a password is and what a hacker will already know or check first), a brute-force attack on a PIN (usually 4 numeric digits) would take 1.7 × 10-12 × 104, or about 17 nanoseconds. On a lower-case-and-numbers password with 7 characters (like "hunter2"), it would take 1.7 × 10-12 × 367, or about 133 milliseconds. On a 26-character password with upper- and lower-case letters and symbols (like "Welcome to the party, pal!"), it's 1.7 × 10-12 × 7026, or 1.6 × 1036 seconds, or about 50 octillion years (that's over 3.5 quintillion times the age of the universe!). And that's not even hard to remember.
  3. Whatever you do, don't use the same password here that you use at your bank.
